Habit 1 — Be Proactive

Take charge of your life.

Being proactive means focusing on what you can influence and making choices according to values rather than moods. Proactive people take initiative, plan ahead and accept responsibility for their actions.

Practical tip: Each morning, list one action you can take today toward a goal — no excuses, just one step.

Habit 2 — Begin with the End in Mind

Define your vision and goals.

This habit asks you to clarify your long-term vision. When you know where you're going, daily choices align with your core values and purpose.

Practical tip: Write a 1–3 sentence personal mission statement that captures who you want to be and what you want to prioritize.

Image ideas: Unsplash (vision & goals)

Habit 3 — Put First Things First

Prioritize what matters most.

Once priorities are clear, schedule and protect the time for them. Use tools like the Eisenhower matrix to separate urgent tasks from important ones and focus on long-term value.

Practical tip: Plan your week around 3 high-impact priorities and block time for them on your calendar.

Image ideas: Unsplash (time management)

Habit 4 — Think Win-Win

Seek mutual benefit in relationships.

Win-Win is an attitude of abundance: there’s enough success for everyone. Approach negotiations and collaborations looking for solutions that benefit all parties, building trust and sustainable relationships.

Practical tip: In team discussions, ask “How can we structure this so everyone wins?” and document agreed outcomes.

Image ideas: Unsplash (teamwork) Official: Habit 4 (FranklinCovey)

Habit 5 — Seek First to Understand, Then to Be Understood

Listen with empathy.

Empathetic listening means understanding another's perspective before offering your own. This builds rapport and opens the door to more productive conversations.

Practical tip: Practice paraphrasing: start replies with “If I understand you correctly, you’re saying…”

Image ideas: Unsplash (active listening)

Habit 6 — Synergize

Together is better.

Synergy is creative cooperation: combining diverse strengths to produce results greater than individual efforts. Welcome different viewpoints and design collaborative processes that amplify results.

Practical tip: Run structured brainstorming sessions where ideas are combined into hybrid solutions.

Image ideas: Unsplash (collaboration)

Habit 7 — Sharpen the Saw

Renew yourself regularly.

Sharpening the saw means regular renewal in four areas: physical, mental, emotional and spiritual. Continuous renewal sustains long-term effectiveness.

Practical tip: Schedule weekly renewal blocks: exercise, learning, connection and reflection.
